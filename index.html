Introduction

Presented here are two graphs. The right graph shows 40 data points. The left graph shows a series of x and y coordinates derived from the outline of a hand. Each data point on the right graph represents the first and second prinicpal component of one of 40 different hands.

Methods

The visualizations are easy to use. Simply hovering over one of the data point on the right graph will update the left graph with the hand that cooresponds to that data point.

Results

Observations (hover over each observation to visualise it above):

One observation to make is that there is no coorelation between the index value of a hand and it"s position on the graph. For instance, the hand created with the points at index value of 5, does not necessarily look similar or is positioned simlar to the hand of index value 4 or 6. Another observation can be made regarding the size of the various hands. As the points move from left to right in the PCA graph, the thickness and length of the hands increases. A final observation is that there appear to be a couple of PCA outliers, the topmost and the bottommost points. The hands that are associated with these point have fingers that are positioned significantly different than the others. While most of the hands are positioned in a somewhat neutral resting state, the two outlier hands have some fingers close together and other farther apart.
