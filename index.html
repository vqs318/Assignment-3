Introduction

Presented here are two graphs. The right graph shows 40 data points. The left graph shows a series of x and y coordinates derived from the outline of a hand. Each data point on the right graph represents the first and second prinicpal component of one of 40 different hands.

Methods

The visualizations are easy to use. Simply hovering over one of the data point on the right graph will update the left graph with the hand that cooresponds to that data point.

Results

One observation to make is the points in the right graph have a strong positive coorelation. Another observation can be made regarding the size of the various hands. Hands with higher values of principal components in both the X and Y coordinates appear to be bigger is size and have the fingers closer together than points will a lower value for X and Y. A final observation is that there appear to be outliers from the general linear coorelation between the X and Y coordinates.
